UNDERSTANDING FAIR VALUE GAPS (FVGs)

A Fair Value Gap is an unfilled price zone where:
- Price jumped, leaving a gap
- Gap represents institutional activity
- ICT: Price will eventually fill this gap (or not)

BULLISH FVG (Gap Up)
  Pattern:
    Bar 1: Up bar
    Bar 2: Up bar with high above Bar 1 close
    Result: Gap between Bar 1 high and Bar 2 low
  
  Interpretation:
    ✓ Buyers are aggressive
    ✓ No sellers at these prices
    ✓ Creates support (FVG low)
  
  Trading:
    - Support level = FVG low
    - Buy when price retests support
    - Stop below FVG
    - Target above FVG high

BEARISH FVG (Gap Down)
  Pattern:
    Bar 1: Down bar
    Bar 2: Down bar with low below Bar 1 close
    Result: Gap between Bar 1 low and Bar 2 high
  
  Interpretation:
    ✓ Sellers are aggressive
    ✓ No buyers at these prices
    ✓ Creates resistance (FVG high)
  
  Trading:
    - Resistance level = FVG high
    - Short when price retests resistance
    - Stop above FVG
    - Target below FVG low

FVG RETEST
  Definition: Price returns and touches the FVG
  Probability: 70-80% of FVGs get retested
  Timing: Usually 1-50 bars after FVG forms
  
  Trading Implication:
    - No retest yet = FVG still valid
    - Retest with confirmation = Strong entry
    - Retest and bounce = Best case

FVG DISPLAY MODES

ALL
  Shows: Every FVG regardless of trend
  Use: When: Learning or high timeframes
  Best for: Research and understanding

TREND_ALIGNED (Recommended)
  Shows: Only FVGs matching current trend
  - Bullish FVGs in uptrend
  - Bearish FVGs in downtrend
  Use: Most trading strategies
  Best for: High-probability setups only

STRICT
  Shows: Trend-aligned FVGs only
  - Plus: Must meet specific close conditions
  - Fewer but higher quality FVGs
  Use: Advanced traders
  Best for: Conservative entries

SUPER_STRICT (ICT Recommended)
  Shows: Highest quality FVGs only
  - Trend-aligned
  - No break opposite direction
  - Specific close conditions
  - Fewest but highest probability
  Use: Professional traders
  Best for: Maximum accuracy (50-70% reduction in signals)

SUPPORT & RESISTANCE FROM FVGs

Each FVG generates S/R lines:

BULLISH FVG → SUPPORT
  ✓ Level: FVG low (bottom of gap)
  ✓ Meaning: Buyers defended this level before
  ✓ Trading: Buy at support

BEARISH FVG → RESISTANCE
  ✓ Level: FVG high (top of gap)
  ✓ Meaning: Sellers defended this level before
  ✓ Trading: Sell at resistance

MINIMUM TRADE FRAMEWORK (mTFW)
  Definition: Expected move target from S/R line
  
  Equities: Based on 40 ticks estimation
  Futures: 40 ticks per contract minimum
  Forex: 15 pips minimum move
  
  Calculation: From S/R level, add mTFW to get target

TROUBLESHOOTING

Q: "No FVGs detected"
A: 1. Use correct timeframe (3-5 min, not hourly)
   2. Ensure price data is clean (no gaps)
   3. Market may be range-bound (no FVGs)
   4. Try less strict FVG mode

Q: FVGs not retesting?
A: 1. Some FVGs won't retest (that's OK - 20-30% normal)
   2. Retest may take hours or next day
   3. Check if price filled gap on different timeframe
   4. FVG may be on wrong side of structure

Q: Too many false signals?
A: 1. Use STRICT or SUPER_STRICT mode
   2. Increase trend alignment requirement
   3. Add volume confirmation filter
   4. Require multiple confirmations

Q: Can't find the three sessions?
A: 1. Check timezone (must be NY time)
   2. Verify timestamps are correct
   3. Not all sessions may exist in data
   4. Weekend data won't have sessions
