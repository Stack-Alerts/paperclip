TTrades framework. It detects precise 4-candle patterns where price
fails a breakout and reverses with expansion confirmation.

THE 4-CANDLE STRUCTURE

CANDLE 1: CONTEXT
  Role: Establishes the range
  Characteristic: Previous swing high or low
  Duration: Can be 1 bar or part of larger structure
  Interpretation: Defines breakout reference level

CANDLE 2: REVERSAL (The Key Signal)
  Role: Fails the breakout
  Bullish Requirements:
    - C1 is DOWN (close < open)
    - C2 trades BELOW C1 low
    - C2 closes ABOVE C1 close
  Bearish Requirements:
    - C1 is UP (close > open)
    - C2 trades ABOVE C1 high
    - C2 closes BELOW C1 close
  
  Interpretation:
    ✓ One side attempted to break
    ✓ Absorption and failure occurred
    ✓ Opposite side gaining control
    ✓ High probability reversal setup

CANDLE 3: EXPANSION (The Confirmation)
  Role: Validates reversal with expansion
  Bullish Requirement: Closes ABOVE C2 high
  Bearish Requirement: Closes BELOW C2 low
  
  Interpretation:
    ✓ New direction has momentum
    ✓ Confirms C2 reversal wasn't false
    ✓ Followers piling in same direction
    ✓ Setup is now confirmed

CANDLE 4: CONTINUATION
  Role: Reinforces the directional bias
  Requirement: Continues in same direction as C3
  
  Interpretation:
    ✓ Momentum sustained
    ✓ No reversal back into C3
    ✓ Trend has legitimacy

UNDERSTANDING EQUILIBRIUM ZONES

Equilibrium zones are support/resistance areas where price acceptance
occurred after the reversal. They're critical for identifying bounce
levels and continuation targets.

CANDLE 2 ZONE (Between C1 and C2 close)
  Bullish: Between C1 low and C2 close
  Bearish: Between C2 close and C1 high
  
  Meaning:
    - Point of reversal
    - First level price may test
    - Buyers/sellers defended this area

CANDLE 3 ZONE (Between C2 and C3)
  Bullish: Between C2 high and C3 high
  Bearish: Between C3 low and C2 low
  
  Meaning:
    - Expansion zone
    - Strong acceptance level
    - Primary target level

WICK THRESHOLD (Default: 75%)
  Definition: If wick > 75% of candle range, use wick for zone
  Reason: Long wicks = rejection, creates tighter zones
  Adjustment:
    - Increase for range-bound markets (75-80%)
    - Decrease for trending markets (60-70%)

  1. Candle 2 condition confirmed (reversal)

ENTRY LOGIC

  Entry Method 3: Breakout above/below C3
  
  Recommended: Wait 1 bar after C3 confirms, enter on pullback to C2 zone

STOP-LOSS PLACEMENT
  Bullish: Below C2 zone (by 0.5-1% margin)
  Bearish: Above C2 zone (by 0.5-1% margin)
  
  Why: C2 zone marks the failure point - breaking below it invalidates setup

TARGET IDENTIFICATION
  Primary Target: C3 zone (first likely target)
  Secondary Target: Next swing high/low
  Trailing Target: Trail stop above/below C2 zone
  
  Expected Move: 1.5x to 3x the C1 range

CONFIGURATION & SETTINGS

WICK THRESHOLD % (Default: 75%)
  Definition: Wick must be X% of total range to use wick for zone
  Higher (80-90%):
    ✓ Tighter zones
    ✓ More selective
    ✗ Fewer patterns
    Use: Volatile markets, looking for precision
  
  Lower (60-70%):
    ✓ More zones detected
    ✓ Broader entry areas
    ✗ Less precise
    Use: Range-bound markets, higher probability

DETECT CANDLE 2 (Default: ON)
  What: Enable C2 reversal detection
  Impact: Without this, no reversals detected
  Recommendation: Always ON

DETECT CANDLE 3 (Default: ON)
  What: Require C3 expansion confirmation
  Impact: OFF = faster signals, lower quality
  Recommendation: ON for swing trading, OFF for scalping

REVERSAL FILTER (Default: OFF)
  What: Only signals at recent extremes
  Lookback: Default 20 bars
  Impact: 30-40% fewer signals, much higher quality
  Recommendation: ON for daily/4H, OFF for 1H/5min

TROUBLESHOOTING

Q: "No patterns detected"
A: 1. Market may be trending (fewer reversals)
   2. Check wick threshold (may be too high)
   3. Reversal filter too strict (try looser)
   4. Data quality - verify OHLC values

Q: "Too many false signals"
A: 1. Enable reversal filter
   2. Require C3 confirmation
   3. Add volume confirmation
   4. Use higher timeframe
   5. Increase wick threshold

Q: "Patterns not matching chart visually"
A: 1. Verify candle definitions (C1 = context correctly?)
   2. Check data alignment (timestamps correct?)
   3. Use daily chart to verify manually
   4. Compare with TradingView Candle 2 Closure

Q: "Zone calculations seem off"
A: 1. Check wick threshold % (default 75%)
   2. Adjust for your market (volatile=lower, calm=higher)
   3. Verify equilibrium zone source (wick vs full range)
   4. Visual comparison on chart
