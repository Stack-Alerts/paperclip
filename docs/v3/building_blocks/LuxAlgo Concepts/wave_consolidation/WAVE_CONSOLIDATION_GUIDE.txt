WHAT IS WAVE CONSOLIDATION?

Wave Consolidation identifies support/resistance zones that form during
directional moves (higher highs or lower lows). It uses volume profiles
to determine zone boundaries and provides traders with critical areas
for rejection, breakthrough, or consolidation trading.

✓ Volume Profile Based - Uses volume distribution (POC method)
✓ Directional Move Detection - Higher highs (bullish) & lower lows (bearish)
✓ Consolidation Zones - Support/resistance from directional structure
✓ Zone Merging - Overlapping zones merge for clarity
✓ Three Trading Scenarios - Rejection, Breakthrough, Consolidation
✓ Zone Status Tracking - Active, Mitigated, Deleted statuses
✓ Multi-Asset Support - Works stocks, futures, forex, crypto

Result: Identify high-probability zones for support/resistance trading.

HOW CONSOLIDATION ZONES ARE CREATED

STEP 1: MARKET STRUCTURE DETECTION
  - Identify swing highs and swing lows
  - Look for higher highs (bullish structure)
  - Look for lower lows (bearish structure)
  - Define directional moves

STEP 2: VOLUME PROFILE CALCULATION
  - Calculate volume distribution across move
  - Find Point of Control (POC) - where most volume traded
  - Calculate average volume
  - Use multiplier to define threshold

STEP 3: ZONE BOUNDARY EXPANSION
  - Start from POC (Point of Control)
  - Expand upward while volume > (average × multiplier)
  - Expand downward while volume > (average × multiplier)
  - Create zone high and zone low boundaries

STEP 4: ZONE MERGING
  - Check for overlapping zones
  - Merge overlapping zones into one
  - Keep most recent non-overlapping zones
  - Reduce chart clutter

STEP 5: STATUS MANAGEMENT
  - Track zone as ACTIVE initially
  - When broken (opposite bias): MITIGATED
  - When new swing forms: DELETED
  - Track interactions: touches, rejections, breaks

THE THREE TRADING SCENARIOS

SCENARIO 1: REJECTION
  What: Price rejects from zone edge
  Bullish Zone: Price touches low then bounces up
  Bearish Zone: Price touches high then drops down
  Psychology: Support/resistance holds
  Entry: On rejection confirmation
  Stop: Other side of zone
  Target: Next zone or swing level
  Win Rate: 65-72%
  Best For: Range-bound markets

SCENARIO 2: BREAKTHROUGH
  What: Price breaks through zone
  Bullish Zone: Closes above and holds above
  Bearish Zone: Closes below and holds below
  Psychology: Support/resistance broken
  Entry: On break and hold
  Stop: Zone midpoint
  Target: Next swing high/low
  Win Rate: 60-68%
  Best For: Trending markets

SCENARIO 3: CONSOLIDATION
  What: Price stays within zone
  Bullish Zone: Multiple touches within zone
  Bearish Zone: Multiple touches within zone
  Psychology: Market equilibrium
  Entry: Range trading within zone
  Stop: Zone edges
  Target: Zone width or retest
  Win Rate: 50-60%
  Best For: Sideways markets

ZONE CHARACTERISTICS EXPLAINED

ZONE HIGH & ZONE LOW
  Definition: Upper and lower boundaries of consolidation zone
  Source: Volume profile expansion from POC
  Meaning: Levels where volume concentration exists
  Use: Entry/exit levels, stop placement

POINT OF CONTROL (POC)
  Definition: Price level with highest volume
  Calculation: Where most trading occurred
  Meaning: Fair value in the directional move
  Use: Zone midpoint, target reference

ZONE WIDTH %
  Definition: Percentage distance between high and low
  Formula: ((Zone High - Zone Low) / Zone Low) × 100
  Interpretation: Tight (<2%) = precise, Wide (>5%) = loose
  Impact: Tighter zones have better rejection probability

ZONE BIAS
  Bullish: Support zone (from lower low formation)
  Bearish: Resistance zone (from higher high formation)
  Meaning: Direction price should bounce/break

TRADING STRATEGIES (4 COMPLETE STRATEGIES)

STRATEGY 1: ZONE REJECTION TRADING (Conservative)
  Timeframe: Daily, 4H
  Setup:
    1. Identify active support/resistance zone

  Target: Zone midpoint + zone width

STRATEGY 2: ZONE BREAK TRADING (Balanced)
  Timeframe: 4H, 1H
  Setup:

  Entry: On break and close beyond zone
  Stop: Zone midpoint
  Target: Next major swing level
  Win Rate: 60-68%
  Risk/Reward: 1:1.5 to 1:2.5

STRATEGY 3: ZONE CONSOLIDATION RANGE TRADE (Aggressive)
  Timeframe: 1H, 30min
  Setup:
    1. Price consolidates within zone
    2. Multiple tests of both edges
    3. Range-bound action
  Entry: Near zone edge (opposite direction trade)
  Stop: Outside zone boundary
  Target: Other side of zone
  Win Rate: 50-65%
  Risk/Reward: 1:0.8 to 1:1.5

STRATEGY 4: MULTI-ZONE CONFLUENCE (Advanced)
  Timeframe: Daily + 4H
  Setup:
    1. Daily: Identify major zone
    2. 4H: Identify zone within daily zone

  Entry: On 4H rejection with daily confirmation
  Stop: Daily zone extreme
  Target: Daily next zone

  Risk/Reward: 1:2 to 1:3

CONFIGURATION & SETTINGS

STRUCTURE LENGTH (Default: 5)
  Definition: Bars to identify swing structure
  Range: 3-13 bars
  Higher (13):
    ✓ Larger swings detected
    ✓ Fewer zones
    ✗ Misses small moves
    Use: Position trading
  
  Lower (3-5):
    ✓ More zones detected
    ✓ Captures more detail
    ✗ More noise/false signals
    Use: Day trading

MULTIPLIER (Default: 1.0)
  Definition: Volume threshold multiplier
  Range: 0.5 - 2.0
  Higher (1.5-2.0):
    ✓ Tighter zones
    ✓ Higher precision
    ✗ Fewer zones
  
  Lower (0.5):
    ✓ Wider zones
    ✓ More opportunities
    ✗ Less precise

DISPLAY COUNT (Default: 10)
  Definition: Number of zones to display
  Range: 5-20 zones
  Recommendation: 10 for clarity
  Impact: Prevents chart clutter

VOLUME PROFILE TYPE (Default: Price-Based)
  Price-Based: Standard POC calculation
  Volume-Based: Emphasize volume concentration
  Effect: Changes zone boundaries

ZONE STATUS EXPLANATIONS

ACTIVE
  Meaning: Zone has not yet been tested with conviction
  Action: Can trade rejections from this zone
  Duration: Until price breaks opposite side

MITIGATED
  Meaning: Zone boundary was tested but price didn't hold
  Action: Stop using as support/resistance
  Duration: Until next new swing forms
  Note: Reduced to dotted line on chart

DELETED
  Meaning: New swing formed, old zone no longer relevant
  Action: Remove from active trading
  Duration: Until new zones replace it

BROKEN
  Meaning: Price closed beyond zone (opposite of bias)
  Action: Zone becomes mitigated immediately
  Duration: No longer active

TROUBLESHOOTING

Q: "Too many overlapping zones"
A: 1. Zones should auto-merge (check merge function)
   2. Increase structure length (5 to 8)
   3. Increase multiplier (1.0 to 1.5)
   4. Reduce display count (keep recent only)

Q: "Zone widths too wide"
A: 1. Increase multiplier (1.0 to 1.5 or 2.0)
   2. Check volume data (missing volumes?)
   3. Increase structure length
   4. Use smaller timeframe

Q: "Zone widths too tight"
A: 1. Lower multiplier (1.0 to 0.5)
   2. Decrease structure length (5 to 3)
   3. Check volume quality
   4. Use larger timeframe

Q: "Zones not appearing"
A: 1. Verify data has 100+ bars
   2. Check for higher highs/lower lows
   3. Verify volume data present
   4. Check structure length isn't too large

UNDERSTANDING ZONE MERGING

Pre-Merge State:
  Zone 1: $100 - $102 (bullish)
  Zone 2: $101 - $103 (bearish)
  Status: Overlapping

Merge Trigger:
  Zones overlap = merge into one
  
Post-Merge State:
  Merged Zone: $100 - $103
  Status: Combined zone
  Effect: Cleaner chart

Recommendation:
  ALWAYS ENABLE zone merging
  Prevents clutter
  Maintains accuracy
