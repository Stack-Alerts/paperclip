# Bullish/Bearish Trend Detection & XGBoost Optimization Guide

## Part 1: Trend Detection Checklist (1H, 2H, 4H Timeframes)

To programmatically determine a trend, avoid relying on a single condition. Use a **scoring system**. If 3 out of 5 conditions are met, the trend is confirmed.

### 1. Moving Average Alignment (Trend Baseline)
The relationship between short-term and long-term averages is the standard baseline.
- **Bullish Check:**
  - Price > 50 EMA
  - 50 EMA > 200 EMA
  - **Slope Check:** `(EMA50_current - EMA50_previous) > 0`
- **Bearish Check:**
  - Price < 50 EMA
  - 50 EMA < 200 EMA
  - **Slope Check:** `(EMA50_current - EMA50_previous) < 0`

### 2. Market Structure (Price Action)
Indicators lag; price structure is the reality of the chart.
- **Bullish Check (Higher Highs/Lows):**
  - Identify pivots (local mins/maxs) over the last 20 candles.
  - Condition: `Current_Pivot_Low > Previous_Pivot_Low` AND `Current_Pivot_High > Previous_Pivot_High`.
- **Bearish Check (Lower Highs/Lows):**
  - Condition: `Current_Pivot_Low < Previous_Pivot_Low` AND `Current_Pivot_High < Previous_Pivot_High`.

### 3. RSI Regime (Momentum)
RSI is not just for overbought/oversold; it indicates the "regime" of the market.
- **Bullish Regime:** RSI(14) > 45. (In strong uptrends, RSI tends to bounce at 40-50 and stay above it).
- **Bearish Regime:** RSI(14) < 55. (In strong downtrends, RSI rarely breaks above 60).
- **Invalid/Chop:** RSI oscillating strictly between 45 and 55 suggests no trend.

### 4. Ichimoku Cloud (Filter)
Use the Cloud (Kumo) as a binary filter.
- **Bullish Check:** Price Close > Span A AND Price Close > Span B.
- **Bearish Check:** Price Close < Span A AND Price Close < Span B.
- **Neutral:** Price is inside the cloud.

### 5. ADX (Trend Strength)
This prevents your script from identifying a "trend" during a flat sideways market.
- **Valid Trend Check:** `ADX(14) > 25`.
- **Instruction:** If ADX < 25, return `TREND_NEUTRAL` regardless of EMAs.

## Part 2: XGBoost Deep Research (Fixing the 53% Accuracy)

If you are stuck at ~53% accuracy, you are likely facing the **Random Walk Hypothesis**. Predicting whether the *very next* candle will be Green or Red is nearly impossible (50/50 chance).

To reach 65-85% accuracy, you must change **what** you are predicting.

### 1. The "Triple Barrier" Method (Target Labeling)
Do not predict `Close[t+1]`. Instead, define success as a volatility event.

**Algorithm:**
For every timestamp `t`, set 3 barriers:
1.  **Upper Barrier:** `Price[t] * (1 + target_percentage)` (e.g., +1%)
2.  **Lower Barrier:** `Price[t] * (1 - stop_loss_percentage)` (e.g., -1%)
3.  **Vertical Barrier:** `t + max_holding_time` (e.g., 24 candles)

**Labels for XGBoost:**
- **Class 1 (Buy):** Price hits Upper Barrier *before* Lower Barrier or Time Limit.
- **Class -1 (Sell):** Price hits Lower Barrier *before* Upper Barrier or Time Limit.
- **Class 0 (Neutral):** Time Limit reached without hitting either barrier.

**Why this works:**
XGBoost learns to identify **high-probability setups** and ignores the Class 0 "chop". Your accuracy on the *predicted trades* (Class 1 or -1) will skyrocket, even if the model stays silent (Class 0) 60% of the time.

### 2. Stationarity (Feature Engineering)
XGBoost cannot predict price levels it has never seen (e.g., if BTC goes to $100k). You must feed it relative data.

**Bad Features (Do not use):**
- Raw Open, High, Low, Close (e.g., 65000.00)
- Raw Volume

**Good Features (Use these):**
- **Log Returns:** `np.log(Close / Close.shift(1))`
- **Distances:** `(Close - EMA50) / Close`
- **Rolling Volatility:** `Close.rolling(window=20).std()`
- **RSI / MFI:** (Already stationary between 0-100)
- **Volume Change:** `(Vol - Vol_MA) / Vol_MA`

### 3. Meta-Labeling
This is the "secret sauce" of high-frequency funds.
1.  Use your **Trend Script** (from Part 1) to generate potential entry signals.
2.  Train XGBoost to predict **"Was the Trend Script correct?"**
    - Input: Volatility, Spread, Volume.
    - Output: 1 (Trade was profitable), 0 (Trade failed).
3.  **Execution:** Only take the Trend Script trade if XGBoost predicts `Probability > 0.7`.

### 4. Evaluation Metric
Stop using "Accuracy". In trading, 55% accuracy with a 2:1 Risk/Reward ratio is a fortune. 90% accuracy with huge losses on the 10% is bankruptcy.
- Optimize for **Precision** (False Positives are expensive).
- Use **Sharpe Ratio** of the equity curve generated by the model's backtest.
